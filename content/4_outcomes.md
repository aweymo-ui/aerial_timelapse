## Conclusion

**As an outsider to GIS**, I was struck by the complexity of creating these visualizations, especially given the challenge of integrating datasets from various departments, federal organizations, and governments, each with its own metadata standards and digitization methods. 

Drawing from this experience, for a more out of the box approach that will be less intimidating to students and researchers, ArcGIS World Imagery Wayback is an excellent, intuitive and fairly new option I would recommend, despite the relatively shallow historical reach of the tool.

For more advanced users, Google Earth Engine feels like the tool with the greatest potential to excel at this and much more complicated requests, although someone with greater understanding of the differences between institutional datasets and a more adept Javascript coder could improve on these efforts.
